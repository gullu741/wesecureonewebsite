# The Rise of Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS)

Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) has fundamentally transformed the cybercrime landscape. By lowering the barrier to entry, sophisticated ransomware operators now lease their malware to "affiliates" who conduct the actual attacks.

## How RaaS Works

1. **The Operators**: Develop the ransomware, maintain the payment portals, and manage the leak sites.
2. **The Affiliates**: Purchase access to the ransomware, compromise target networks, and deploy the payload.
3. **The Initial Access Brokers (IABs)**: Often, affiliates purchase pre-existing network access from IABs, skipping the difficult reconnaissance phase.

## Defending Against RaaS

Defending against RaaS requires a multi-layered approach:

- **MFA Everywhere**: Multi-Factor Authentication is the single most effective control against credential-based attacks.
- **Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)**: Traditional antivirus is no longer sufficient. EDR provides visibility into behavioral anomalies.
- **Immutable Backups**: Ensure that your backups cannot be encrypted or deleted by an attacker who has gained administrative access to your network.
