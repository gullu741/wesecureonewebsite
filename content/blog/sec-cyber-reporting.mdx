# Navigating the New SEC Cyber Reporting Requirements

The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) has fundamentally changed the stakes of cybersecurity for public companies. Cybersecurity is no longer just an IT issue; it is a material financial risk that must be disclosed with the same rigor as quarterly earnings.

## The 4-Day Rule

The most significant change is the requirement for companies to disclose a "material" cybersecurity incident within four business days of determining that it is material. This puts immense pressure on internal incident response teams to:
- Conduct rapid impact assessments.
- Accurately determine materiality (a legal and financial threshold).
- Coordinate with legal and communications teams for a public filing (Form 8-K).

## Annual Disclosures (Regulation S-K)

Beyond incident reporting, companies must now describe in their annual 10-K filings:
1. Their processes for identifying and managing cybersecurity risks.
2. The board of directors' oversight of those risks.
3. Management's role and expertise in assessing and managing material cybersecurity threats.
