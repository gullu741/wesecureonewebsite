# The Shifting Landscape of Cyber Insurance

Gone are the days when a simple questionnaire was enough to secure a $10M cyber insurance policy. Following a record year of ransomware payouts, insurers have significantly raised their standards.

## The High Cost of Insecurity

Many organizations are seeing premium increases of 50% to 100% year-over-year. Even worse, some are being denied coverage entirely because they lack "baseline" controls like:
- **EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response)**
- **MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication) on all external access**
- **Hardened, offline backups**

## The Underwriting Shift

Underwriters are now performing their own external scans and requesting proof of regular penetration testing and vulnerability management. They want to see that your organization is proactive, not just reactive.

## Securing Your Coverage

To ensure you remain insurable at reasonable rates, we recommend:
1. **Gap Assessments**: Conduct an audit specifically aligned with common insurance requirements.
2. **Prioritized Remediation**: Fix the high-impact issues (like lack of MFA) immediately.
3. **Continuous Monitoring**: Prove you have "eyes on glass" 24/7/365.

Cyber insurance is a safety net, but a strong security posture is what keeps you from falling into it.
